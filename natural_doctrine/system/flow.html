Gameplay Flow

On the World Map you can confirm the name of your current location, as well as information about it. There are two types of missions: Story Missions will advance the story, while Free Missions will let you gain extra experience and items. If you need more gear or levels before advancing the story, use these to prepare.


On the World Map, you can also change your unit formation, outfit your allies with equipment, and acquire new skills. Also, the World Map is the only place you can save your game.

You can play through Free Missions more than once, but you can only do each mission a certain number of times in a row. Also, the further you delve into each stage, the more powerful the enemies will become.

When a stage begins, you will see its victory conditions. With those in mind, review the stage layout, the ally and enemy lineup, and the turn order, then decide how best to approach the battle. Victory won't always come simply by destroying the enemy - sometimes you'll have to reach a certain area, or escape the stage entirely. So pay close attention to what you need to do to win!




Once the battle begins, select whichever viewpoint gives you the best grasp of the situation. You can see the ally and enemy turn order at the top of the screen, so use your viewpoint to move your units around and keep an eye on the enemy. You can highlight a unit to see its status details on the right side of the screen, and confirm its turn order via the flashing icon at the top of the screen.
















Link Turn allows some or all of your units to combine and attack at the same time. However, if you use it improperly, you'll leave yourself open to continuous attacks from the enemy. In other words, it's a powerful double-sided blade.





 
When allies are in the same or adjacent area, the chance of a Link Turn goes up. If a Link Turn is possible, the icons of any allies that can link will appear at the bottom of the screen.


 
As you input commands for each ally in the Link Turn, their icon will appear in the turn order at the top of the screen.






 
In this picture, the player has set up a 4-unit Link Turn. You can see what effect will be created by the attack and command combinations.


 
When performing a Link Turn, any unit that participated will have their turn moved to the end of the Turn Order. As a result, you open yourself up to a barrage of continuous enemy attacks. This can get you in big trouble, so it's important you pay close attention to how you use this technique.























If even one character falls in NAtURAL DOCtRINE, it's Game Over. You can't save during a mission, so be sure to save early and often on the World Map!



You can set your difficulty level when you first start the game, and you can modify this at any point during play (even during missions).
